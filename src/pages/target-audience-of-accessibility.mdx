# Target Audience of Accessibility

Accessibility (a11y) benefits a wide range of users, not only people with permanent disabilities.

Accessible applications are easier to use, more robust, and provide a better experience for everyone.

## Users with Visual Disabilities

Users who may:

- Be blind
- Have low vision
- Have color vision deficiencies (color blindness)

Common assistive technologies:

- VoiceOver (macOS / iOS)
- NVDA (Windows)
- JAWS (Windows)
- TalkBack (Android)

Accessibility considerations:

- Semantic HTML
- Alternative text for images
- Sufficient color contrast
- Keyboard accessibility
- Screen reader support

---

## Users with Motor Disabilities

Users who may have difficulty using a mouse or performing precise movements.

Examples:

- Tremors
- Limited hand mobility
- Temporary injuries

Accessibility considerations:

- Full keyboard support
- Large click/tap targets
- Avoid drag-only interactions
- Visible focus indicators

---

## Users with Hearing Disabilities

Users who are deaf or hard of hearing.

Accessibility considerations:

- Captions for videos
- Transcripts for audio content
- Visual alternatives to audio-only notifications

---

## Users with Cognitive Disabilities

Users who may experience challenges with memory, attention, reading, or comprehension.

Examples:

- ADHD
- Dyslexia
- Learning disabilities

Accessibility considerations:

- Clear language
- Consistent navigation
- Predictable interactions
- Helpful error messages

---

## Users with Temporary Impairments

Accessibility also benefits users experiencing temporary limitations.

Examples:

- Broken arm
- Eye strain
- Recovering from surgery

Accessibility considerations:

- Keyboard alternatives
- Flexible interaction methods
- Clear content structure

---

## Users in Situational Limitations

Users may encounter accessibility challenges due to their environment.

Examples:

- Bright sunlight
- Noisy surroundings
- One-handed mobile usage
- Poor internet connection

Accessibility considerations:

- Good contrast
- Captions
- Responsive layouts
- Robust user interfaces

---

## Why Accessibility Matters

Accessibility helps ensure that everyone can:

- Perceive content
- Operate interfaces
- Understand information
- Successfully complete tasks

Building accessible applications improves usability, inclusivity, and overall user experience for all users—not just people with disabilities.
